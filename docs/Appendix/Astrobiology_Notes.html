  • $ \(*Terrestrial * Planets*\) $
    • Earth and Venus happen to be the two largest with defined atmospheres.
    • Earth and Mercury have an internally generated magnetic field.
    • Mercury has the thinnest atmosphere, Venus has the thickest.
  • $ \(*Minor * Planets * and * Comets* \) $
    • The Kuiper belt consists of a thick disk of small bodies beyond the orbit of Neptune. These bodies are composed of a mixture of ice and rock, where the two largest members are Eris and Pluto (see what’s going on here?) A minor planet include asteroids, as well as distant minor planets.
    • Comets are ice-rich objects that shed mass when subjected to solar heating. Comets may have formed near the giant planet region initially, were transported and stored in the Oort Cloud, Kuiper Belt, or the scattered disk.
  • $ \(Satellite * and * Rings* Systems\) $
    • Mercury and Venus are the only two planets in the solar system without natural satellites.
    • All major satellites (except Triton) orbit in the direction of the host planet’s rotation (i.e. prograde).
    • Venus and Uranus are the only two planets that move in retrograde.
    • Each of the giant planets have rings!
  • $ \(Heliosphere\) $
    • The planetary orbits that lie within the region of space containing the Sun’s magnetic fields and ionized gas. The solar wind consists of plasma that could set our tech back by a hundred years at least.
  • $ \(What * is * a * Planet?\) $
    • Planet : A celestial body that orbits the Sun, has a sufficient mass to assume a nearly round shapeshape through its self-gravity, and has cleared its orbital neighborhood of similar sized-bodies.
    • Dwarf Planet : A celestial body that orbits the Sun, has sufficient mass to assume a (nearly round) shape through its self-gravity, has not cleared its orbital neighborhood of similar sized-bodies, and is not a satellite.
    • Star : A self-sustaining (proton; proton chain) fusion is sufficient for thermal pressure to balance gravity.
    • Stellar remnant : no more fusion (dead star)
    • Brown dwarf : substantial deuterium (proton + neutron) fusion, where more than of the object’s original deuterium inventory is destroyed by fusion.
    • Planet : negligible fusion, with a precise mass limit depending on the initial composition. It can orbit one (or more) stars, a stellar remnant, or no stars at all.

  • $ \(Minor * Planets * and * Comets\) $
    • The Kuiper belt is a thick disk of small bodies, it is mainly composed of rock and ice.
    • Eris and Pluto are the largest of the bodies in the Kuiper belt.
    • Pluto and Neptune have a crossing orbit, but never collide.
    • The objects in the Kuiper belt can only be observed at their perihelion due to darkness.
    • Comets - ice rich objects that can originate from the Asteroid belt or the Kuiper belt.
